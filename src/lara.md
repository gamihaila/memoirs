It was fall of 1996. I had just arrived back in Toronto from France
and was getting ready for the new school year. One of the activities
was an orientation session at the International Student Center. I had
not attended in the past but now, as a fresh single, I was determined
to get out there and meet new people. OK, I was looking to meet
girls. After the new experiences in Paris I was more emboldened to
approach strangers and make acquaintances. It was the ideal
environment for it too, since everyone there was a new student,
typically from another country, and they were therefore open to making
new friends. As a somewhat senior student I had stuff to talk about
with the newcomers. That helped a lot, as I was normally rather shy.

I had a longer conversation with one girl called Jenny, a
Canadian. Later we became friends, but never more than that. Meeting
her proved to be very impactful to my life, but in a very unexpected
way. Let me explain how. A month later, on Canadian Thanksgiving,
another girl I met during the same orientation party invited me to go
with her and some other friends to a Thanksgiving dinner at a Catholic
church. I wasn’t a churchgoer at the time so I was reluctant to go but
she insisted and the meal was just $5 so I decided to go.

At that meal I saw someone at the other end of the large dinner hall
who looked familiar. Thinking it was Jenny I waved to her and she
smiled and waved back. I got up from our table and started walking
towards her. As I got closer I realized it was not Jenny, but she
continued to smile looking at me. When I got there I introduced myself
and apologized for my mistake.

“No worries! I’m Lara, by the way,” she said, and continued chatting
with me. “Nice meeting you, I’ll see you around,” I said after a few
minutes of chitchat.

She mentioned casually she went to the campus gym on Tuesdays and
Thursdays at 10. Naturally I went there too the next Tuesday and sure
enough I saw her there but just nodded to her, as she was working out
on a treadmill. To my surprise she came over to me after her workout
and gave me her phone number.

I called her the next day and invited her to see a play that I had
already seen before[^ The Clouds, by Aristophanes]. It was a musical
comedy adaptation by a local troupe. I offered to come pick her up
from her home and she started giving me driving directions.

“I don’t have a car,” I said, embarrassed. “Oh, no worries, I live
right near a subway stop, and it’s anyway easier to go by transit.”

I was relieved. So I went to her house, and we went to see the play
together. During the play I took her hand. She didn’t pull back. I
was on cloud nine! I walked her home afterwards and as I was saying my
goodbyes, she invited me in. She cooked a pasta dish for us, and we
ate it sitting on the floor. All the time she kept talking and asking
me about my life. She was an amazing listener. After dinner she played
Debussy for me on her grand piano, beautifully. I was immediately and
thoroughly caught under her spell!

After that wonderful evening we started dating right away and a few
weeks later she moved in with me. Soon after she asked me if I still
keep in touch with Ioana and if it would be OK if she called her. I
gave her Ioana's phone number and she called her from my phone:

"Hello, is this Ioana?", she opened.

"Yes, who is this?", anwered Ioana.

"Hi, you don't know me, but I do. My name is Lara and I'm dating your
ex-husband George", she introduced herself.

"Oh?"

"Do you mind if I ask you a few questions about George?"

"Not at all, go ahead", agreed Ioana, taken by surprise.

"Listen", said Lara, "he asked me to marry him on our first date".

"Oh, yes", Ioana answered, "he did the same with me. He totally means
it, you know, that's just how he was raised".

"I see", said Lara, "I didn't know what to think about that, we had
just met".

"No, no, he's serious. He knows right away.", Ioana assured her.

"So, listen", continued Lara, "you left some of your nighgowns in the
closet, do you want them back?"

"No", answered Ioana surprised.

"Then I'll wear them, they are really comfy! And thanks for George as
well, he's really nice to me!"

"You're welcome!", laughed Ioana.

If this conversation sounds surreal to you, you're not alone. I was
having a blast listening to them. At the time, still fairily new to
the country I thought this must be normal, like a new employer asking
for references from your past employers. I'm sure Ioana had a good
laugh too. Not too late after this conversation they met each other in
person at a friend's wedding we were both invited (actually it was me
and Lara who introduced him to Lara's best friend and they got
married, imagine that!). Lara made an extra effort to gaze lovingly
into my eyes while slow dancing making sure Ioana can see us on the
dance floor. Then she went to her and thanked her again for the
nightgowns and for the boyfriend (!). She was like that, never letting
a funny situation go unexploited.


We were together for three years. It wasn’t a smooth ride though; she
moved out and rented her own apartment a few weeks into our
relationship, because she needed a place to retreat to whenever she
wanted. We would be together for the whole evening and suddenly she
felt what she would describe as an “attack” and she had to leave. She
was back the next day, happy and smiling as if nothing had happened.

At some point during the Fall of 1997 I got an email from Dana, who
was now working at Bell Labs with Alon Halevy, and living with her
husband in a townhouse in New Jersey. She was inviting us to spend the
winter vacation at her house: "You can go to the city anytime by
train, we're only half an hour away", she said. When I told Lara, she
was initially excited to go visit New York, but then she started
worrying if she will be able to handle being together for that long,
given her attacks. Up until we got into the train she was going back
and forth, not sure whether she could go. Luckily the train pulled off
just as she was about to get up and leave, so she stayed, but her face
was breaking off. Too late to get off, so she endured the twelve hour
ride somehow. We got off in Penn Station and took a local train to New
Jersey. Dana and her husband Luis came to pick us up from the station
with their car. Luis proved to be very good at putting Lara at ease
with his conversation skills, and Lara was surprisingly good at making
small talk. She had an entirely different personality when she was
among strangers, just like playing a character in a play.

The next day we took the train to New York and spent the day walking
around and filming with my brand new camcorder. I recorded our
conversations where she was telling me that just half a pill of
Rivotril was all it takes for her to love me. She was serious. We went
to Central Park and sat on a bench and talked. She told me she would
not be able to marry me, but that she's happy to hang out for as long
as I wanted, until I found someone to marry. "Even after", she said,
"I would move accross the street from you and your wife and you can
come and see me whenever you want". 

Needless to say, the back-and-forth emotional roller coaster was
exhausting. I had no choice, however; I was smitten with her. Many
years later, when I watched a movie[^ The Eternal Sunshine of the
Spotless Mind, 2004] I identified with the main character.
